AE Follow-Up Talk Track

For Attendees — Conversation Starters

  1. "What was your take on Bob's point about starting with data before agents?"
    Opens the door to understanding where they are in their AI journey. If they agree, you're aligned. If they push back, you learn what's driving their urgency.
  2. "Are you seeing the agent sprawl problem he described — multiple AI pilots that don't connect?"
    Positions Agent Fabric as the answer. Most orgs have 3-5 disconnected AI efforts. This question surfaces their pain.
  3. "Which part of the session felt most relevant to what your team is working on right now?"
    Let them tell you where to focus. Their answer tells you whether to lead with MuleSoft (integration), Data 360 (data), or Agentforce (AI agents).

For No-Shows — Key Insight + Offer

Lead with this insight:

"The biggest takeaway from the session was Bob Heyl explaining that the orgs getting real ROI from AI agents aren't the ones with the best models — they're the ones who built the data and integration foundation first. He called it 'feeding the agent clean data instead of asking it to clean the data.' That one line landed with the whole audience."

Then offer: "I've got the recording — happy to send it over. Or if you'd rather, I can walk you through the 3 key points in 15 minutes and we can see if any of it maps to what you're working on."

Objection Handlers

"We're already invested in [competitor] for integration."
"Makes sense — most of the organizations in the session have existing integration tools. MuleSoft doesn't require rip-and-replace. The value of this approach is that it connects what you already have into a unified data layer that agents can actually use. Bob's team at CareFirst kept their existing systems and layered MuleSoft on top. Want me to show you what that looks like in a 30-minute demo?"
"We're not ready for AI agents yet."
"That's actually the smart position. Bob's whole point was that you shouldn't start with agents — you start with clean, connected data. If you get the data foundation right now, you're ready for agents whenever the business is. The organizations that skip that step end up rebuilding later. Would a deeper look at the data and integration layer — separate from the AI piece — be useful?"
"We don't have budget for new platforms right now."
"Totally fair. One thing that came up in the session was the ROI timeline — CareFirst saw value in months, not years, because they started with high-impact integration use cases that pay for themselves. Things like automating prior auth data exchange or consolidating patient data across systems. If I could show you a use case that has a clear payback in Q1, would that change the budget conversation?"
